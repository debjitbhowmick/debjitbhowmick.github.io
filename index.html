About Me

As a transport engineer and data scientist, I’m passionate about using data-driven insights to shape sustainable and community-focused transport solutions. With over five years of experience across academia and industry, I specialise in transport modelling, demand estimation and forecasting, and project management for active and shared transport initiatives. My expertise lies in interpreting complex data and translating it into strategic recommendations for policymakers that enhance infrastructure efficiency and improve community travel choices. I’m skilled in working with diverse stakeholders—ranging from government agencies to private partners—to foster collaboration and achieve shared goals in transport planning. Currently, as a Research Fellow at Monash University, I lead projects focused on active transport, leveraging tools like Python, GIS, and agent-based modelling to deliver impactful insights. I am excited about the future of transport and look forward to connecting with professionals who are equally passionate about making our cities more sustainable and livable.

Projects

CYCLED STUDY - City-wide bicycling exposure modelling study

The CYCLED (CitY-wide biCycLing Exposure modelling) Study aims to develop a world-leading platform for city-wide modelling of cycling exposure. This will provide unparalleled insights into cycling exposure by combining multiple cycling data sources through the use of advanced spatial statistical and machine learning techniques. Overall, we anticipate the use of these data will result in improved safety for cyclists, lower injury rates, increased cycling participation and reduced inequities.

Link to project website
Developing a bicycling infrastructure classification system for Greater Melbourne, Australia using OpenStreetMap

OpenStreetMap (OSM) is a valuable open-source map database that contains transport infrastructure data among other things and has spatial coverage for almost the entire planet. Hence, it is used extensively by researchers and planners and it helps develop methods that are transferable and thus can be replicated irrespective of the study area. I developed a classification process to classify existing bicycling infrastructure across Greater Melbourne, Australia. We have derived knowledge from existing studies and calibrated our classification system to suit local tagging practices.

Consulting

I have contributed to multiple transport modelling projects involving signalised and priority junctions, model calibration/validation, and traffic survey data analysis. My work has supported clients in making informed, evidence-based infrastructure decisions.

Key Contributions
  • Modelled priority junctions and roundabouts using Junctions9 and AutoCAD, and signalised junctions using LinSig, adhering to Highways England standards.
  • Calibrated and validated LinSig models following the Model Auditing Process as per Transport for London guidelines.
  • Analysed traffic count data in MS Excel to identify peak hours and vehicle volumes.
  • Extracted saturation flows, degrees of saturation, and underutilised green time from video surveys.
  • Analysed travel time outputs from VISSIM models and merged networks in VISUM.
  • Generated technical reports and maintained quality assurance documentation.
  • Collaborated with transport planning teams across India and the UK, managing project tasks, resolving modelling issues, and delivering final deliverables.
