About Me

I’m a transport engineer and data scientist with a passion for using data-driven insights to shape sustainable, community-focused transport systems.

With over five years of experience spanning academia and industry, I specialise in transport modelling, demand forecasting, and project management for active and shared mobility initiatives.

I excel at transforming complex mobility data into clear, strategic recommendations that help policymakers improve infrastructure and empower community travel choices.

I’ve worked closely with government bodies, private consultancies, and interdisciplinary teams to drive collaborative transport planning outcomes that are both evidence-based and forward-thinking.

Currently, I serve as a Research Fellow at Monash University, leading projects on active mobility. I regularly apply tools like Python, GIS, and agent-based modelling to deliver actionable insights.

I’m enthusiastic about the future of sustainable transport and always keen to connect with others working to make cities more liveable and equitable.

Projects

CYCLED STUDY - City-wide bicycling exposure modelling study

The CYCLED (CitY-wide biCycLing Exposure modelling) Study aims to develop a world-leading platform for city-wide modelling of cycling exposure. This will provide unparalleled insights into cycling exposure by combining multiple cycling data sources through the use of advanced spatial statistical and machine learning techniques. Overall, we anticipate the use of these data will result in improved safety for cyclists, lower injury rates, increased cycling participation and reduced inequities.

Link to project website
Developing a bicycling infrastructure classification system for Greater Melbourne, Australia using OpenStreetMap

OpenStreetMap (OSM) is a valuable open-source map database that contains transport infrastructure data among other things and has spatial coverage for almost the entire planet. Hence, it is used extensively by researchers and planners and it helps develop methods that are transferable and thus can be replicated irrespective of the study area. I developed a classification process to classify existing bicycling infrastructure across Greater Melbourne, Australia. We have derived knowledge from existing studies and calibrated our classification system to suit local tagging practices.

Identifying optimal policies to increase active travel uptake

This project will co-design a policy decision-support tool with project partners, allowing governments to evaluate different scenarios and prioritise investments to maximise equitable active travel uptake. The tool will help decision-makers identify the most cost-effective policies to increase active travel uptake, aligned with their strategic goals and desired outcomes. It will provide partners with a practical, evidence-based resource to guide investment across Victoria, supporting broader health, sustainability, liveability and equity goals.
